## Changes
*For exact before/after text, see [psalm_060_copy_edit_diff.md](psalm_060_copy_edit_diff.md).*

1. [7, 9] **Introduction**: Revised the explanation of the Hebrew perfect from "the tense of the deed that is finished and cannot be undone" to "the form that typically marks what is presented as done," and softened "every single perfect-tense verb" to "the completed actions are overwhelmingly God's."  
   The original description treated the perfect too rigidly as a tense and overstated the claim; the revised wording preserves the grammatical insight without making a demonstrably inaccurate linguistic claim.

2. [7] **Introduction**: Corrected "the same five-letter word" for אֱדוֹם to "the same four-letter word."  
   אֱדוֹם is a four-letter Hebrew word, so the original count was factually wrong.

3. [2, 7] **Introduction**: Changed "The oracle's center is relentlessly singular" to "The psalm's center is relentlessly singular," and clarified the Ketiv/Qere note to "written ועננו, which could be read 'answer us,' but read וַעֲנֵנִי."  
   יֹבִלֵנִי occurs outside the oracle proper, so "oracle's center" was too narrow; the Ketiv/Qere wording also needed to match the later, more precise discussion.

4. [7] **Verse 1**: Replaced "by the third century BCE" with "by the time of the Greek translators."  
   The Psalms portion of the LXX is not securely dated that early, so the original date was too specific for the evidence.

5. [7] **Verse 2**: Changed "the lone perfect of human accomplishment" to "the lone completed verb of human victory."  
   וַיַּךְ is not a perfect form, so the grammatical label was wrong even though the broader point about completed human victory could be retained.

6. [1] **Verse 3**: Revised "It frames the whole complaint as an inclusio" to "It brackets the psalm's lament."  
   The repeated זְנַחְתָּנוּ returns in v. 12, outside the defined complaint section of vv. 3–5, so the original scope of the inclusio claim was too narrow and structurally imprecise.

7. [7] **Verse 3**: Corrected Lamentations 2:7 from "God spurning his sacrifice" to "God spurning His altar."  
   Lamentations 2:7 speaks of the altar, not a sacrifice, so the original citation misdescribed the text.

8. [8] **Verse 3**: Removed the stem label "Polel" while retaining the interpretive point that תְּשׁוֹבֵב is an intensive form.  
   The stem name itself added grammatical jargon without doing interpretive work for this audience.

9. [3] **Verse 4**: Reworded "the lexical fracture enacts the geological one" to "the lexical novelty matches the geological one."  
   The observation depends on the word's rarity, not on a formal feature literally performing the meaning, so the effect needed to be attributed more accurately.

10. [9] **Verse 5**: Replaced "The psalmist is consciously drinking from the prophetic tradition" with a non-chronological description of the biblical cup-of-wrath idiom.  
   The original phrasing implied a specific literary dependence and chronology that the paragraph did not establish.

11. [8] **Verse 8**: Removed the stem label "Piel" from the discussion of אֲחַלְּקָה.  
   The grammatical label was pure annotation; the interpretive point was about land allotment, not about the stem name itself.

12. [1, 9] **Verse 8**: Changed "a merism for the whole land" to "together they span both banks of the river."  
   The pair Shechem/Sukkoth supports a claim about territory on both sides of the Jordan, but the stricter label "merism" was not adequately earned.

13. [9] **Verse 9**: Changed "Each name then receives a piece of royal equipment" to "The final two names then receive pieces of royal equipment."  
   In the verse, only Ephraim and Judah receive the imagery of equipment; Gilead and Manasseh are simply claimed as possessions.

14. [7, 9] **Verse 9**: Corrected "the Chronicles genealogy" to Judges 5:14 and simplified the Torah Temimah sentence to a general note about rabbinic play on מְחֹקֵק.  
   The original misattributed the verse and drew an opaque conclusion about rabbinic argumentation that readers could not follow from the text as written.

15. [6, 9] **Verse 10**: Scaled back the sandal argument from "This is legal gesture, not mere insult" and "therefore a deed of title" to a claim about legal overtones alongside humiliation, and removed the Nuzi-tablets sentence.  
   The evidence cited supports possession imagery, but not such a categorical conclusion; the outside parallel also was not needed to sustain the point.

16. [7] **Verse 10**: Corrected the LXX gloss from "I will subdue the foreigners" to "foreigners have been subjected to me."  
   The Greek ἐμοὶ ἀλλόφυλοι ὑπετάγησαν is not first-person future active, so the original rendering was inaccurate.

17. [6, 9] **Verse 10**: Scaled back the Amrita Pritam comparison so it highlights a limited contrast in the language of division rather than claiming that "every divine division of land is... a division of people."  
   The original conclusion overreached the evidence and turned a suggestive comparison into a sweeping claim the paragraph had not established.

18. [5, 7] **Verse 12**: Replaced the direct Hebrew quotation in the commentary with the English phrase "with our hosts."  
   The running-text Hebrew form was inaccurate, but the standalone verse line could not be altered under the formatting rules, so the commentary was adjusted instead.

19. [7] **Verse 14**: Softened the pun claim from "יָבוּס is consonantally the Jebusite" and "whom David trampled" to a more accurate echo with Jebus/Jebusite, whom David displaced.  
   The original wording overstated both the exactness of the pun and the historical analogy.
