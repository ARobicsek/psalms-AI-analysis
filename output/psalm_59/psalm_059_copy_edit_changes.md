## Changes
*For exact before/after text, see [psalm_059_copy_edit_diff.md](psalm_059_copy_edit_diff.md).*

1. [2][7] **Introduction**: Corrected the opening claim that the refrain appears "word for word" and that "not a vowel changes" to note that the second occurrence adds a prefixed waw and a slight vowel shift.  
   The original was factually wrong and contradicted the later discussion at **Verse 15**, which correctly notes the small formal difference between vv. 7 and 15.

2. [2][7] **Introduction — The architecture**: Corrected the translation of עֻזּוֹ אֵלֶיךָ אֶשְׁמֹרָה from "my strength" to "his strength — toward You I keep watch."  
   The Hebrew in v. 10 is third person עֻזּוֹ, not first person עֻזִּי; the original mistranslated the verse and conflicted with the later paragraph on the "grammatical knot" in **Verse 10**.

3. [2][7][9] **Introduction — One word fought over by three parties / power paragraph**: Changed "in vv. 10–11" to "in vv. 5, 10–11" and changed "God's עֻזּוֹ" to neutral "עֻזּוֹ, 'his strength'."  
   The original verse range excluded v. 5 even while citing וּרְאֵה from v. 5, and "God's עֻזּוֹ" prematurely decided a debated reading that the commentary itself later presents as ambiguous.

4. [2][9] **Introduction — concluding paragraphs**: Changed "the speaker is granted the one verb that can" to "the verbs that can," and removed the claim that חַסְדִּי is unambiguously "the speaker's, claimed at last as his own."  
   The original singular "one verb" did not fit the commentary's own emphasis on multiple first-person forms, and the claim about חַסְדִּי overstated a point the later commentary treats as textually and semantically double.

5. [CITATION FIX] [7] **Verse 1**: Corrected the reference from 1 Samuel 19:11 to 1 Samuel 19:11–12 for the quotation וַתֹּרֶד מִיכַל אֶת־דָּוִד בְּעַד הַחַלּוֹן.  
   The quoted words occur in v. 12, not v. 11; the original citation mislocated the quoted Hebrew.

6. [2][7] **Verse 3**: Revised "they do אָוֶן; he is without it" to "they do אָוֶן; he is without the near-sounding עָוֺן."  
   The original treated אָוֶן in v. 3 and עָוֺן in v. 5 as the same word, but the commentary itself later distinguishes them as near-homophones with different consonants and meanings.

7. [7] **Verse 5**: Changed "invisible to anyone reading consonants alone" to "invisible to anyone hearing the verse aloud without seeing the consonants."  
   The original was self-contradictory: the difference between אָוֶן and עָוֺן is visible in the consonants but can disappear in sound.

8. [2][7] **Verse 7**: Changed "the verbatim refrain" to "the near-verbatim refrain."  
   The refrain is not strictly verbatim because v. 15 has וְיָשֻׁבוּ rather than יָשׁוּבוּ; the correction aligns this paragraph with the commentary's later accurate notice of the difference.

9. [6][9] **Verse 8**: Softened the Plath comparison from "takes up precisely the enemies' false premise" to a more limited analogy about speech not vanishing into air.  
   The original claim overstated the specificity of the parallel; Plath's poem does illuminate the lingering force of words, but not with the exact premise the commentary originally assigned to it.

10. [2][7] **Verse 15**: Changed "repeated unchanged," "The form is identical," and "the two identical pictures" to "repeated almost unchanged," "The form is almost identical," and "the two almost identical pictures."  
   The original language contradicted the actual wording of vv. 7 and 15 and the commentary's own later observation about the small but real difference between them.

11. [7][9] **Verse 16**: Changed "The picture mirrors Amos exactly" to "The picture closely recalls Amos."  
   The original overclaimed the intertextual precision; Amos 4:8 provides a real verbal parallel, but not an exact mirror of Psalm 59:16.
