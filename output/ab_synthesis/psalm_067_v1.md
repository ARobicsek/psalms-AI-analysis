## GOVERNING OBSERVATION

**Claim:** The psalm's grammatical *person* shifts in lockstep with its concentric shape: the frame-verses (v2, v7, v8) speak ABOUT God in the third person and ask blessing for "us" (jussive "may God…," "his face," "him," "our God"), while the entire central block (v3–v6) turns to direct second-person address OF God ("your way," "your salvation," "praise YOU," "YOU judge," "YOU guide"). Moving inward is thus a move from talking about God on Israel's behalf to talking to God as the nations' object of praise, then back out — and the shift is reinforced phonetically, since the first-person-plural suffix נוּ ("us") occurs only in the frame.

**Verses it spans:** v2, v3, v4, v5, v6, v7, v8

**Evidence (per verse, name the actual Hebrew):**
- v.2: third-person jussives אֱלֹהִים יְחָנֵּנוּ / יָאֵר פָּנָיו, with three "us" suffixes — יְחָנֵּ֥נוּ, וִיבָרְכֵ֑נוּ, אִתָּ֣נוּ
- v.3: switch to second person — דַּרְכֶּ֑ךָ, יְשׁוּעָתֶֽךָ ("your way / your salvation")
- v.4: direct address + vocative — יוֹד֖וּךָ … אֱלֹהִ֑ים ("praise YOU, O God")
- v.5: second-person verbs — תִשְׁפֹּ֣ט ("you judge"), תַּנְחֵ֣ם ("you guide")
- v.6: second-person address repeated — יוֹד֖וּךָ
- v.7: back to third person + the only "us" suffixes return — יְבָרְכֵ֗נוּ אֱלֹהִ֥ים אֱלֹהֵֽינוּ
- v.8: third person — יְבָרְכֵ֥נוּ … וְיִֽירְא֥וּ א֝וֹת֗וֹ ("may they revere HIM")

**Novelty check (why a learned reader of Psalms would not already know this):** The concentric structure is well documented, but that the person-of-address (3rd→2nd→3rd) and the "us"-suffix sound both flip exactly at the structural seams is not a standard observation.

## CORE OBSERVATION 1

**Claim:** The nations' response to God is staged across the whole psalm as a three-step ascent of verbs: cognition → acclamation → awe. They first come to *know* (v3), then to *praise* (vv4, 6), and finally to *revere* (v8) — a deliberate escalation that only appears when the three response-verbs are read together.

**Verses it spans:** v3, v4, v6, v8

**Evidence (per verse, name the actual Hebrew):**
- v.3: לָדַ֣עַת — "that [it] be known" (root ידע, intimate knowing)
- v.4 / v.6: יוֹד֖וּךָ — "they praise you" (root ידה, public acclamation)
- v.8: וְיִֽירְא֥וּ — "they fear/revere" (root ירא, existential awe)

**Novelty check:** The praise→fear escalation is noted at v8, but folding in v3's לָדַעַת to make a three-stage cognition-to-awe arc is a cross-verse construction.

## CORE OBSERVATION 2

**Claim:** The psalm deploys three different words for "peoples," and their distribution is structurally keyed rather than random: גּוֹיִם appears only once, at the first naming of the nations (v3); the rare/archaic לְאֻמִּים is reserved for the structural center (v5, twice, and nowhere else); and the common עַמִּים carries the refrains (vv4, 6) and the pivot's judgment clause (v5). The center is the one place where two of the three terms collide.

**Verses it spans:** v3, v4, v5, v6

**Evidence (per verse, name the actual Hebrew):**
- v.3: בְּכׇל־גּ֝וֹיִ֗ם — the sole גּוֹיִם, opening the nations-theme
- v.4: עַמִּ֥ים … עַמִּ֥ים כֻּלָּֽם (refrain)
- v.5: both terms — תִשְׁפֹּ֣ט עַמִּ֣ים … and the only לְאֻמִּים: יִ֥שְׂמְח֥וּ … לְאֻ֫מִּ֥ים, וּלְאֻמִּ֓ים בָּאָ֖רֶץ
- v.6: עַמִּ֥ים … עַמִּ֥ים כֻּלָּֽם (refrain, matching v4)

**Novelty check:** That all three peoples-terms are present and that the archaic לְאֻמִּים is confined to the tricolon center while גּוֹיִם is confined to the opening is a distributional pattern not visible verse-by-verse.

## CORE OBSERVATION 3

**Claim:** The root ברך ("bless") forms an asymmetric envelope: a single petition opens the psalm (v2) and a doubled, back-to-back petition closes it (vv7–8), with no occurrence in the central nations-block — and the only possessive divine name, אֱלֹהֵינוּ ("our God"), surfaces inside that closing double-blessing. The blessing-of-"us" is the outer shell; universal praise fills the hollow center.

**Verses it spans:** v2, v7, v8

**Evidence (per verse, name the actual Hebrew):**
- v.2: וִיבָרְכֵ֑נוּ — one blessing-verb in the opening petition
- v.7: יְ֝בָרְכֵ֗נוּ אֱלֹהִ֥ים אֱלֹהֵֽינוּ — blessing-verb plus the lone covenantal possessive
- v.8: יְבָרְכֵ֥נוּ אֱלֹהִ֑ים — second, immediately consecutive blessing-verb

**Novelty check:** The 1×-front / 2×-back asymmetry of ברך, and the coincidence of "our God" with the doubled rear blessing, is a whole-psalm pattern, not a per-verse fact.

## CORE OBSERVATION 4

**Claim:** The psalm's spatial reach widens monotonically from intimate nearness to maximal distance, tracked by a recurring אֶרֶץ thread: God's face is asked to be "with us" (v2), then his way/equity acts "on the earth" (vv3, 5), the earth yields its produce (v7), and finally the "ends of the earth" revere him (v8). The opening locus is God's face beside the worshippers; the closing locus is the planet's farthest margins.

**Verses it spans:** v2, v3, v5, v7, v8

**Evidence (per verse, name the actual Hebrew):**
- v.2: פָּנָ֖יו אִתָּ֣נוּ — face "with us," maximal proximity
- v.3: בָּאָ֣רֶץ — "on the earth"
- v.5: בָּאָ֖רֶץ — "on the earth" (guidance of nations)
- v.7: אֶ֭רֶץ נָתְנָ֣ה — the earth as giver
- v.8: כׇּל־אַפְסֵי־אָֽרֶץ — "all the ends of the earth," maximal distance

**Novelty check:** The אֶרֶץ thread is flagged at v8, but reading it as a continuous near-to-far spatial vector anchored by פָּנָיו אִתָּנוּ at the start is a cross-verse synthesis.

## ADDITIONAL OBSERVATION 1

**Claim:** The opening verb of divine shining and the closing verb of nations' fearing share the consonants aleph and resh in reversed order, bracketing the psalm with a light-to-awe sound-play: יָאֵר ("may he shine") opens, יִירְאוּ ("may they revere") closes.

**Verses it spans:** v2, v8

**Evidence (per verse, name the actual Hebrew):**
- v.2: יָ֤אֵֽר (root אור: y-prefix, aleph, resh)
- v.8: וְיִֽירְא֥וּ (root ירא: y-prefix, resh, aleph) — the same aleph/resh, metathesized

**Novelty check:** This is a consonantal mirror (not homophony) linking the psalm's first divine action to its last human response, which no verse-level reading would surface.

## ADDITIONAL OBSERVATION 2

**Claim:** The psalm is almost entirely paratactic petition and declaration; its single subordinating causal connective, כִּי ("for"), sits at the exact structural center (v5), making the pivot the one place the poem states a reason — the nations' joy is grounded because God judges with equity.

**Verses it spans:** v5 (against the petition-only texture of v2–v4, v6–v8)

**Evidence (per verse, name the actual Hebrew):**
- v.5: כִּֽי־תִשְׁפֹּ֣ט עַמִּ֣ים מִישֹׁ֑ר — the lone causal clause, at the tricolon center

**Novelty check:** That the poem's only "because" coincides with its only tricolon and its structural midpoint is a pattern visible only when the whole verb/clause texture is held together.

## ADDITIONAL OBSERVATION 3

**Claim:** Against a verb system that is wholly jussive/imperfect (petition and confident future), exactly one finite verb is a perfect — נָתְנָה ("has given," v7) — so the single completed action in the psalm is the earth's already-accomplished yield, planting one realized fact amid a poem of requests.

**Verses it spans:** v2, v7 (and the jussive texture throughout)

**Evidence (per verse, name the actual Hebrew):**
- v.2: יְחָנֵּנוּ … יָאֵר (jussive petitions, representative of the whole)
- v.7: נָתְנָ֣ה — the sole qatal perfect, the one completed deed

**Novelty check:** The tense anomaly is noted at v7, but framing it as the *only* perfect against an otherwise uniformly modal verb system is a whole-psalm count.

## ADDITIONAL OBSERVATION 4

**Claim:** Selah falls after v2 and after v5 but not after v6, even though v6 verbatim repeats v4 — so the two pause-markers land on the frame-opening petition and on the weighted center, not on the recurring refrain, suggesting Selah here tracks structural weight rather than the refrain that would seem its natural host.

**Verses it spans:** v2, v5, v6

**Evidence (per verse, name the actual Hebrew):**
- v.2: סֶֽלָה after the opening petition
- v.5: סֶֽלָה after the central tricolon
- v.6: no סֶלָה, despite being identical to v4

**Novelty check:** The asymmetric placement only becomes meaningful when v4 and v6 are recognized as identical yet differently marked — a comparison across non-adjacent verses.
