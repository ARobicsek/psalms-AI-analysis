## Changes
*For exact before/after text, see [psalm_058_copy_edit_diff.md](psalm_058_copy_edit_diff.md).*

1. [7, 9(e)] **Introduction**: Changed "spends ten verses begging God to destroy" to "turns to spend four verses begging God to destroy." The original overstated the scope of the imprecation; the direct destruction-requests are concentrated in vv. 7–10, not in ten verses.

2. [7] **Introduction; Verse 5**: Removed the claim that אטם is "the same word used for sealing a wall or a document" and the supporting references. The cited passages use other roots, so the examples were factually wrong; the sentences were revised to preserve the point that the verb is active and deliberate.

3. [9(b)] **Liturgical — Key verses and phrases, Verse 9**: Softened "The puzzle answers itself" and "The 'song' was forced by lexicography" to hedged wording. The uniqueness of שַׁבְּלוּל supports a plausible explanation for *Perek Shirah*'s choice, but it does not prove that motive with certainty.

4. [7, 9(e)] **Verse 1**: Revised the מִכְתָּם paragraph to say that all six psalms are Davidic, with 56–60 clustered around persecution. The original implied that Psalm 16 belonged to the same persecution-cluster, which overreached the evidence.

5. [7] **Verse 2**: Corrected the statement that the Septuagint turns the line "first-person" to "second-person." The cited Greek verb λαλεῖτε is second-person plural.

6. [9(b)] **Verse 4**: Changed "beyond the reach of correction, by definition" to "portrayed as beyond the reach of correction." Estrangement from birth does not logically entail incorrigibility as a definition; the stronger claim needed softening.

7. [6, 9(c)] **Verse 5**: Toned down the Ezekiel comparison for כִּדְמוּת. The doubled phrasing can suggest a heightened, uncanny register, but the wording alone does not sustain the stronger claim that the serpent is lifted into full throne-vision imagery.

8. [7] **Verse 6**: Corrected the Deut 18:11 reference from לֹחֵשׁ to חֹבֵר חָבֶר. The prohibition list in Deuteronomy names the spell-binder of the next colon, not the whisperer.

9. [8] **Verse 6**: Removed the grammatical label "is a passive form" from מְחֻכָּם. The stem label itself was not doing interpretive work beyond the simpler point that the charmer is an expert.

10. [1, 7] **Verse 7**: Replaced the misdescribed "precise chiastic shape" with "symmetrical bracketing" and corrected the element description to include the central teeth/fangs imagery. The original sequence as written did not actually describe a chiasm, and the verse's symmetry is better stated directly. Also removed the incorrect Isa 22:19 example for הֲרֹס.

11. [2, 9(b)] **Verse 9**: Softened "Perek Shirah must assign this verse to the snail" to "may explain why *Perek Shirah* assigns this verse." This restores the hedge appropriate to the earlier lexicographic argument rather than hardening it into certainty.

12. [7] **Verse 9**: Changed "That 'never saw light' is precisely our..." to "matches our..." Job 3:16 and Ps 58:9 share imagery, but they do not use identical wording.

13. [2, 9(b)] **Verse 10**: Reworked the paragraph that treated the expert charmer of v. 6 as part of the wicked's own competence. Earlier analysis had made the charmer a separate figure; the revision keeps the point about human expertise without collapsing distinct roles.

14. [7] **Verse 11**: Corrected the Sotah 13a summary so that Ḥushim is Jacob's grandson, not Esau's, and Esau's eyes fall onto Jacob's lap. The original retelling garbled the relationships and details of the Talmudic scene.
