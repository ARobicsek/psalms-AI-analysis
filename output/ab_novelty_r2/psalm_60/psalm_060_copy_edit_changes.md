## Changes
*For exact before/after text, see [psalm_060_copy_edit_diff.md](psalm_060_copy_edit_diff.md).*

1. [7] **Introduction**: Changed "the longest historical note in the Psalter" to "one of the longest historical notes in the Psalter." — Psalm 60's superscription is lengthy, but it is not clearly the single longest in the Psalter; the original overclaimed a factual superlative.

2. [7, 9(c)] **Introduction**: Revised the textual-crux sentence to say the LXX reads קֶשֶׁת/"bow" and removed the Syriac from that claim; also softened "both carry the consonants קשת" to "both can be linked to the consonants קשת." — Later in the commentary the Syriac is correctly said to back the MT, so the original created an internal factual conflict and stated a conjectural linkage too strongly.

3. [7] **Introduction — The floor plan**: Changed "A petition (vv. 6–7) asks for a banner and the rescue..." to "A transition (vv. 6–7) speaks of a banner and asks for the rescue..." — Verse 6 is not itself a petition for a banner; the original description mischaracterized the content of that section.

4. [7] **Verse 2**: Rewrote the commander/casualty comparison to note different commanders and different totals (David/18,000; Abishai/18,000; Joab/12,000). — The original incorrectly called all three references "the same 12,000-casualty engagement," but 2 Samuel 8:13 and 1 Chronicles 18:12 give eighteen thousand, not twelve thousand.

5. [9(b)] **Verse 2**: Softened "suggests the psalm has detached" to "may suggest the psalm had detached." — Variant attributions can support that inference, but they do not prove it; the conclusion needed a hedge.

6. [7] **Verse 3**: Changed "the same verb that opens the Korahite and Asaphite laments" to "the same verb that appears in Korahite and Asaphite laments." — The cited example (Ps 74:1) is Asaphite; the original wording overstated the pattern for Korahite laments.

7. [8] **Verse 3**: Removed the stem label "Polel" while keeping "rare intensive form of שוב." — The technical stem name was not necessary for the interpretive point and added grammatical jargon without helping the reader.

8. [7] **Verse 4**: Replaced the claim that the LXX's "present ἐσαλεύθη" confirms an ongoing sense with a direct explanation from the Hebrew context. — ἐσαλεύθη is not a present tense form, so the original grammatical support was incorrect.

9. [9(c)] **Verse 5**: Softened "carries an Egyptian undertone" to "may carry an Egyptian undertone." — A shared root with Exodus 1:14 is suggestive but not enough to establish the allusion as certain.

10. [3, 7] **Verse 6**: Changed "pairs a noun with a verb from its own root (נסס)" to "pairs a noun with a cognate verb." — This preserves the form-meaning observation without making an unnecessarily precise root claim.

11. [7, 9(b)] **Verse 6**: Removed the sentences about נסס sitting close to נוס and the concordance sweep of "those consonants," and rewrote the transition into the LXX reading. — The original argument relied on an inaccurate consonantal comparison and a strained sound-based inference that did not convincingly support the conclusion.

12. [9(c)] **Verse 6**: Softened "it lands the verse inside the Davidic tradition..." to "it would place the verse inside the Davidic tradition..." — That connection is interpretive rather than demonstrable and needed to remain hedged.

13. [1, 9(e)] **Verse 8**: Replaced the claim that Shechem and Sukkoth "form a merism" covering "the entire promised land, coast to Transjordan" with the more limited statement that they mark both sides of the Jordan. — Shechem and Sukkoth do not constitute a full-spectrum merism for the whole land, and Shechem is not "the coast"; the original overclaimed the scope.

14. [8, 9(f)] **Verse 9**: Removed the label "participle" from מְחֹקְקִי and deleted the Torah Temimah/Yevamot sentence. — The grammatical label did not drive interpretation, and the rabbinic citation led to an opaque and unconvincing conclusion the paragraph did not explain.

15. [2, 8, 9(f)] **Verse 11**: Rewrote the discussion of יֹבִלֵנִי / נָחַנִי to note the mismatch without insisting on a specific grammatical solution. — The original paragraph was internally inconsistent ("past fact" vs. "precative") and too technical for the point being made.

16. [7, 9(b), 9(e)] **Verse 12**: Replaced the extended argument about the Elohistic Psalter making the title ה׳ צְבָקוֹת "structurally unavailable" with a narrower observation about the poem's only army-language being human. — The original logic did not hold: Elohistic psalms can still use army language for God, and the claim about צבקות as a uniquely divine epithet was overstated.
