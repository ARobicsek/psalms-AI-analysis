## Failure audit

### Commentary A
**OVERREACH:**
- "Every one of them — Rashi, Radak, Meiri, Metzudat David — anchors this psalm to a single scene: David in the night-camp." This is contradicted by A's own v2 note, where Radak and Meiri read אֵלֶם as the *bench of judges* — not the night-camp scene. The unanimity is overstated.
- "The verb here is חזה — the prophet's word for *seeing*… not ordinary רָאָה." חזה is used broadly (incl. mundane sight); the strict "prophetic word, not ordinary seeing" is sharpened beyond what the lexicon supports. The intra-psalm v9/v11 pairing survives regardless, but the gloss is overstated.
- The anatomical chain ("sealed ear → therefore destroy the mouth") is presented as the poem's *logic* rather than as the commentator's reconstruction; elegant but a just-so framing.

**FALSE PROFUNDITY:**
- "justice is not an abstraction handed down from heaven but the literal *re-tuning of the body's organs*… back to honest function" — grand restatement of the thesis already made.
- "the grammar of a poem in a hurry" — cadence over content (though tied to Meiri).

**LOST HEDGES:** The "stolen scale" reading (Prov 16:11) is asserted as the poem's design; it is a strong but inferential link that deserved a "may." (Anchors are real, so this is mild.)
