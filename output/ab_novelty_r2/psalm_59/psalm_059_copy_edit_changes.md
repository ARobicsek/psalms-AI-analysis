## Changes
*For exact before/after text, see [psalm_059_copy_edit_diff.md](psalm_059_copy_edit_diff.md).*

1. [7, 9] **Introduction**: Changed Refrain A from "word for word" / "unchanged" to "almost word for word" / "almost unchanged." The two dog-refrains are not strictly identical, since v. 15 adds an opening conjunction; the structural claim needed to match the actual wording.
2. [7] **Introduction**: Replaced "the same root the Ziphites' counterpart used to watch David's house" with "the same root the superscription used of Saul's men watching David's house." The original reference imported the Ziphites from a different psalm context and was factually wrong for Psalm 59's superscription.
3. [9(f)] **Liturgical — Key verses and phrases**: Softened "later commentators strain to explain it" to "later commentators offer various explanations." The original made an unsourced claim about commentators' difficulty; the revision keeps the point without attributing a judgment the text does not document.
4. [7] **Verse 1**: Changed "the same negated-destruction imperative" to "a similar negated-destruction imperative." אַל־תַּהַרְגֵם is not the same imperative or root as אַל־תַּשְׁחֵת, so the wording needed to avoid implying identical phrasing.
5. [8] **Verse 2**: Replaced "the reflexive-intensive stem of קום" with "a form of קום that suggests self-raising." The grammatical label added jargon beyond what the interpretive point required; the simplified phrasing preserves the insight without unnecessary parsing.
6. [9, 7] **Verse 3**: Changed "a technical term for sorcerers" to "an allusion to sorcerers." The original phrasing overstated a speculative proposal; the revised wording preserves the conjecture while matching the evidential weight.
7. [7] **Verse 6**: Replaced "without the usual construct syntax" with "stacked in rapid sequence." The original claim about syntax was inaccurate, since the divine titles do include construct relationships.
8. [7, 9] **Verse 6**: Changed "the God of Israel's armies is being summoned against the army of Israel's own king" to "the God of armies is being summoned against forces sent by Israel's own king." The original wording overstated both the sense of צְבָקוֹת and Saul's "army" in this scene.
9. [7] **Verse 7**: Changed "the same verb returns word-for-word in verse 15" to "the same verb and line return almost word-for-word in verse 15." As in the introduction, the repeated line is not strictly identical because of the added conjunction.
10. [9(c)] **Verse 9**: Changed "By citing the enthronement psalm" to "By echoing the enthronement psalm." The shared wording strongly suggests resonance with Psalm 2, but "citing" pressed the intertextual claim more strongly than the paragraph demonstrated.
11. [7] **Verse 11**: Corrected the Septuagint citation from "ὁ ἔλεος αὐτός" to "τὸ ἔλεος αὐτοῦ." The original Greek phrase was inaccurate; the revised wording matches the LXX's support for the Kethib sense "his mercy."
12. [9(b)] **Verse 12**: Revised the Cain comparison from "all three Cain elements" to a more careful description of the shared pattern. The original overstated the match by claiming an explicit pedagogical element in Genesis 4 that the text does not state in the same way.
13. [7, 9] **Verse 13**: Rewrote the sentence on יְסַפֵּרוּ to remove the unsupported ambiguity between active "they tell" and passive "they are recounted/numbered." The Hebrew form does not carry the passive meaning the original sentence claimed.
14. [9(b), 9(e)] **Verse 14**: Softened "rule from within the people" to "locating that rule within Jacob's sphere" and replaced the fanciful gloss on אֶפֶס with "marking the extremity." The original interpretation pushed beyond what the wording itself demonstrated.
15. [3, 7, 2] **Verses 15–16**: Replaced "a thin overnight whine" with "the mere fact of spending the night hungry" and softened the comment on אִם־לֹא יִשְׂבְּעוּ. The original attributed an effect to sound/meaning the Hebrew verb does not support and also conflicted with the later correct explanation of לין as "lodge/spend the night."
16. [9(b)] **Verse 17**: Softened the claim about the perfect tense from a certainty ("deliverance declared accomplished because the speaker has decided it is certain") to a more cautious account grounded in proven past help and possible proleptic trust. The original conclusion was stronger than the evidence required.
17. [2, 9(b)] **Verse 18**: Changed "settles the verse-11 crux in favor of the Qere" to "supports the Qere at verse 11." The earlier discussion had honestly framed the reading as an inference; the later formulation hardened that conjecture into certainty and needed to be scaled back.
18. [7] **Verse 18**: Changed "this verse's morning-and-refuge vocabulary" to "the psalm's morning-and-refuge vocabulary." "Morning" belongs to v. 17, not v. 18 alone, so the reference needed to match the actual text.
