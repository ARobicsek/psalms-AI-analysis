## Failure audit

### Commentary A
**OVERREACH**
- *"The enemies are conjugated almost entirely in the imperfect…"* — overstated; the keyword verb אָרְבוּ (v.4) is perfect, as A itself later concedes. The thesis is sound but the word "almost entirely" inflates it.
- Plath "Words" — *"like the psalm's God in the next verse, knows the stroke does not stop where the speaker thinks it does"* — the analogue is decorative; the link is asserted, not earned.
- *"a phrase found nowhere else in the Psalter in exactly this form"* (אֱלֹקֵי חַסְדִּי) — true only because "in exactly this form" does heavy lifting; borderline.

**FALSE PROFUNDITY**
- *"The enemies return forever and arrive nowhere; the man they hunted… reaches it."* — elegant but restates the aspect point already made.
- *"God is the constant; what deepens is the singer's grasp of Him."* — sermonic restatement of the mishgav-fixed observation.

**LOST HEDGES**
- The aspect/grammar architecture is presented as the poet's design rather than as a reading; it deserves "arguably."

### Commentary B
**OVERREACH**
- *"the only thing that finally defeats a hostile noise is a different noise"* — thesis stated as the poem's "deepest claim"; rhetorical overstatement.
- Shimei: *"The hunted man pleads against being made the thing his enemies will one day call him."* — anachronistic (Shimei is later narrative); softened by "hovering… the alert reader will," so only mild.

**FALSE PROFUNDITY**
- *"strength belongs to God to redistribute"* — restates the עz-confiscation point.

**LOST HEDGES**
- B is generally well-hedged ("almost certainly," "contested," "not entirely obvious," "if that resonance is heard"). The Mowinckel sorcery claim is explicitly flagged as contested — a calibration plus, not a failure.
