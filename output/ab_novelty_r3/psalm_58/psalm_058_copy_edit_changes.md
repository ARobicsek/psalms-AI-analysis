## Changes
*For exact before/after text, see [psalm_058_copy_edit_diff.md](psalm_058_copy_edit_diff.md).*

1. [7, 9] **Introduction**: Corrected the Solomon citation and translation to distinguish 1 Kgs 8:27 from 2 Chr 6:18.  
   The original quoted 1 Kings as though it included "with man" and all three terms אֱלֹקִים / אָדָם / בָּאָרֶץ; that wording belongs to Chronicles, so the sentence needed to be made textually accurate.

2. [2, 9] **Introduction**: Changed the unnamed-divine-action span from "(vv. 8–11)" to "(vv. 8–10)" and softened "God is the unnamed 'he'" to "God is the likely unnamed 'he'."  
   The essay itself had already assigned vv. 11–12 to the verdict's aftermath, so including v. 11 in the curse-cascade created an internal inconsistency and overstated the claim.

3. [9] **Liturgical — Full psalm**: Softened "make it unusable as communal prayer" to "have made it a poor fit for communal prayer."  
   The liturgical absence may suggest difficulty of use, but it does not by itself prove absolute unusability.

4. [2] **Liturgical — Full psalm**: Changed "The most striking full appearance" in **Perek Shirah** to "The most striking later reuse."  
   The paragraph discusses only verse 9, not the whole psalm, so the original phrasing contradicted its own evidence.

5. [7, 9] **Verse 2**: Revised the claim about הַאֻמְנָם from "consistently in adversarial mouths" to "often appears in skeptical or challenging questions."  
   The original was too sweeping for the evidence offered; the softer claim matches the cited examples without overclaiming the word's usage.

6. [7] **Verse 2**: Corrected the Septuagint description from "shifted to the first person" to "recast the line as straightforward second-person address."  
   The Greek λαλεῖτε is second-person plural, so the original grammatical description was simply wrong.

7. [7] **Verse 3**: Replaced inaccurate lexical support for פלס with accurate parallels involving פֶּלֶס / פלס.  
   Prov 16:2 and Job 31:6 do not use the root under discussion, so the original citations did not actually support the claim being made.

8. [1] **Verse 4**: Changed "inclusio" to "birth-imagery arc."  
   Verse 4 and verse 9 do not repeat the same opening and closing word or phrase, so this is not an inclusio in the technical sense.

9. [6, 9] **Verse 5**: Removed the Ezekiel throne-vision comparison and rephrased the effect of כִּדְמוּת more modestly.  
   The original intertextual claim rested mainly on a shared word and pushed the evidence further than it could bear.

10. [8, 9] **Verse 6**: Scaled down "the most emphatic construction Hebrew offers" to "an emphatic construction."  
   The original was grammatical overstatement and added more jargon-weight than the point required.

11. [7] **Verse 6**: Revised the Septuagint note from the too-specific "φαρμακός, 'drug-maker / poisoner'" to broader "φάρμακα language, associated with potions and spells."  
   The original description was too narrow and potentially misleading about the Greek wording and its effect.

12. [9] **Verse 6**: Softened "The charmer is the most expert who ever lived" to "The charmer is presented as maximally expert."  
   The original made an unnecessary absolute claim; the revised wording matches what the verse actually demonstrates.

13. [1] **Verse 9**: Changed "inclusio" to "birth-imagery arc" in the stillbirth discussion.  
   As in verse 4, the relationship is thematic rather than a technical inclusio based on repeated wording.

14. [9] **Verse 9**: Removed the sentence about the Talmudic *ishut* and blindness.  
   The original citation introduced opaque logic that the paragraph did not explain well enough for a first-time reader to follow.

15. [7, 9] **Verse 10**: Corrected the Genesis 50:10 reference by removing the claim that גֹּרֶן הָאָטָד was where Jacob was buried and clarifying the rabbinic linkage.  
   The threshing-floor of Atad is a mourning site on the way to burial, not Jacob's burial place, so the original statement was factually wrong.

16. [7, 9] **Verse 12**: Replaced "the very בְּנֵי אָדָם ... who were the object of corrupt judgment in verse 2" with a more neutral link to the human sphere named there.  
   In verse 2, בְּנֵי אָדָם is syntactically ambiguous and cannot confidently be treated as the object of judgment.

17. [7, 9] **Verse 12**: Revised the discussion of אֱלֹקִים שֹׁפְטִים to note that plural agreement is possible but not the usual pattern, and hedged "three readings run at once" into "leaves room for several resonances."  
   The original overstated both the grammatical norm and the interpretive certainty.

18. [7] **Verse 12**: Corrected Calvin's reference from verse 11 to verse 12.  
   The discussion concerns בָּאָרֶץ in the closing verdict, which is verse 12, not verse 11.

19. [7, 9] **Verse 12**: Corrected the Faiz translation and narrowed the comparison to what the quoted lines actually support.  
   The original English rendering added material not present in the cited Urdu lines, and the broader literary claim went beyond the evidence supplied in the paragraph.
