## Changes
*For exact before/after text, see [psalm_060_copy_edit_diff.md](psalm_060_copy_edit_diff.md).*

1. [7, 9(b)] **Introduction**: Softened the description of στηλογραφία from “the technical name for the engraved victory-monument of the ancient Near East” to “the engraved text of a public monument.”  
   The original wording overclaimed the precision and scope of the Greek term; the evidence supports “stele-inscription,” but not that exact technical genre label.

2. [9(e)] **Introduction**: Changed “reduces the surrounding nations to household objects” to “reduces the surrounding nations to household images of contempt.”  
   Not every image in vv. 8–10 is literally a household object, so the original scope was too narrow and imprecise.

3. [9(b)] **Introduction**: Replaced “We can prove this is deliberate” with “One sign that this is deliberate.”  
   The comparison with Psalm 108 strongly supports the point but does not strictly “prove” authorial deliberateness.

4. [2, 7, 9(e)] **Introduction**: Revised the grammar paragraph to acknowledge the positive perfect נָתַתָּה לִּירֵאֶיךָ נֵּס and to avoid the absolute claim that “only one thing has actually happened.”  
   The original argument overlooked verse 6’s positive perfect and therefore overstated the pattern; the revised wording preserves the insight while matching the actual verb distribution.

5. [7] **Introduction**: Changed “Petra” to “often identified with Edomite Sela/Petra” in the opening synthesis.  
   The identification is common but not certain enough to state without qualification.

6. [7] **Verse 1**: Changed “proof that the phrase had gone dark” to “a sign that the phrase had gone dark.”  
   The Septuagint’s divergent rendering suggests opacity, but “proof” was too strong for the evidence.

7. [7] **Verse 1**: Changed “names a genre — the engraved public monument” to “names a form — public inscription on a stele.”  
   The Greek supports the notion of inscription on a stele; “genre” and “public monument” were too specific.

8. [7] **Verse 1**: Corrected “The same form sits over Hezekiah’s poem” to “A related form sits over Hezekiah’s poem.”  
   מִכְתָּם and מִכְתָּב are cognate but not the same form.

9. [9(b)] **Verse 2**: Changed “three competing memories of a single war” to “three divergent memories of the war.”  
   The original phrasing overstated the certainty that these are competing accounts of one event rather than divergent preserved traditions.

10. [7] **Verse 4**: Removed the claim that “the Septuagint’s present-tense σαλεύω confirms it” and rephrased the sentence around the Hebrew מוט itself.  
   The Greek evidence as cited did not securely support the claim about present-process force, so the argument was grounded instead in the Hebrew wording.

11. [7, 9(c)] **Verse 6**: Replaced the faulty “concordance search on נסס turns up a crowd of flight-forms” explanation with a more careful statement that the phrase can be heard against the sound of נוס and that the Septuagint reflects that hearing.  
   The original conflated distinct roots and made a linguistically inaccurate argument.

12. [7] **Verse 8**: Replaced the stray Russian word “двойственна” with “ambiguous.”  
   The non-English intrusion was an obvious error and obscured the meaning.

13. [7] **Verse 8**: Changed “three first-person cohortatives” to “three first-person verbs.”  
   אֲמַדֵּד is not a cohortative, so the original grammatical description was inaccurate.

14. [1, 9(e)] **Verse 8**: Replaced “form a merism for the whole land” with “together mark territory on both sides of the river.”  
   Shechem and Sukkoth do not clearly constitute a true merism spanning the whole land; the revised wording matches the evidence without overstating scope.

15. [9(f)] **Verse 9**: Removed the sentence about Torah Temimah linking Judah and Gilead through Hezron.  
   The citation’s relevance was not explained clearly enough for a first-time reader to follow the logic.

16. [7] **Verse 10**: Replaced the contradictory Radak quotation about a cooking vessel with a plain statement that he takes the image as a washbasin.  
   The original wording was internally inconsistent and did not accurately support the point being made.

17. [6, 9(c)] **Verse 10**: Removed the Nuzi-tablets sentence and the Stephen Fry parenthesis; softened “is to plant a deed of ownership” to “can suggest planting a deed of ownership.”  
   The Nuzi comparison and modern memoir reference did not add securely grounded illumination here, and the ownership inference needed hedging.

18. [7, 9(d)] **Verse 11**: Changed “Radak and most moderns identify” to “Radak and many moderns identify,” and replaced “contradicting each other” with “stand back to back in raw tension.”  
   The identification is common rather than universal, and promise versus present experience is a tension, not a flat contradiction.

19. [7, 9(f)] **Verses 12–13**: Changed “which replaces the Tetragrammaton with אֱלֹקִים throughout” to “where the Tetragrammaton is characteristically replaced with אֱלֹקִים,” and rewrote “the name they cannot say” as “the war-title the psalm does not use.”  
   The original wording overstated the scope of the Elohistic phenomenon and introduced an anachronistic implication the paragraph had not established.

20. [2] **Verse 14**: Changed “losing both the kinetic trampling and, more importantly, leaving the tense’s open edge intact” to “losing the kinetic trampling while leaving the tense’s open edge intact.”  
   The original sentence was self-contradictory: it said the Septuagint both lost and preserved the same feature.
