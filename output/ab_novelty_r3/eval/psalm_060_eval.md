## Failure audit

### Commentary A
**OVERREACH**
- "Psalm 60 is built like a triumph-monument" — the entire framing rests on one disputed LXX gloss (στηλογραφία) elevated to an interpretive architecture; presented more firmly than the single anchor supports.
- "The cry of fracture shadows the cry of jubilation" (פצם ~ פצח) — a one-consonant sound-leap built on a hapax whose meaning Rashi himself couldn't fix; flagged with "may," but the prose leans hard on it.

**FALSE PROFUNDITY**
- "a banner *bannering itself*" — cadence over content; restates the cognate-accusative.
- "the deed is signed; the door is barred" — pleasant, but only re-says the v.10/v.11 tension already stated.

**LOST HEDGES**
- "lived prayer quietly votes for the singular, royal reading" — the segulah custom is suggestive, not a verdict on a textual crux; worded as settled.

### Commentary B
**OVERREACH**
- "There may be a buried echo: יָבוּס shares its consonants with Jebus" — honestly hedged ("may," "covert"), so mild; the only real reach, and it is correctly fenced.
- "the harder, bolder MT reading is preferable precisely because it goes further" (v.10 הִתְרוֹעָעִי) — "goes further" is rhetorical, not a text-critical criterion; minor.

**FALSE PROFUNDITY**
- "Faith, in Psalm 60, is exactly the distance between God's finished sentence and our unfinished one." — epigram, but it is *earned* by the preceding grammatical demonstration, so borderline rather than empty.
- "The same consonants that produced victory in the heading become the plea against abandonment in the body." — actually carries content (שׁוב), not hollow.

**LOST HEDGES**
- "I would commit to 'truth'" (v.6) — appropriately marked as the author's judgment; no lost hedge. B generally flags its conjectures more cleanly than A.
