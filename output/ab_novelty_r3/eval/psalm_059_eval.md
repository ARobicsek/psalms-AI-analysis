## Failure audit

### Commentary A
**OVERREACH**
- "The poem's deepest claim is that the only thing that finally defeats a hostile noise is a different noise." — totalizing claim about the "deepest" meaning, more slogan than argument.
- "the distance of a single consonant" / "Watching becomes singing" — the שׁ→ז shift is one consonant, but אֶשְׁמֹרָה (qal) and אֲזַמֵּרָה (piel) are not otherwise "near-twins"; the binyan difference is quietly elided to sharpen the effect.
- Shimei irony ("the very accusation Shimei later hurls at David himself") is hedged but still presented as a load-bearing "hard irony"; it is purely anticipatory and external to the psalm.

**FALSE PROFUNDITY**
- "God's reach grows; the dogs shrink; the two motions are one mechanism." — substantive on first read but the last clause adds cadence, not content.
- "the proper response to being watched through the night is, at last, to sing." — restates the thesis as benediction.

**LOST HEDGES**
- Mowinckel's sorcery reading is flagged "contested," good; but the inference "which would make the swords on their lips literal verbal magic" rides on it as if licensed.

### Commentary B
**OVERREACH**
- "a phrase found nowhere else in the Psalter in exactly this form" (אֱלֹקֵי חַסְדִּי) — the "in exactly this form" hedge saves it, but the rhetorical force pushes toward a stronger uniqueness claim than verified.
- חֵמָה "also means 'venom'… answers their poison-lips" — BDB does note the sense, but the psalm names *swords*, not venom; the "answer" is constructed, not in the text.
- Aspect thesis occasionally overstates ("his grammar can never close; his does") — but B honestly concedes the perfect אָרְבוּ in v.4, which keeps it calibrated.

**FALSE PROFUNDITY**
- "The enemies return forever and arrive nowhere; the man they hunted wills one thing… and reaches it." — earns its cadence (it is the actual thesis), borderline.
- "God is the constant; what deepens is the singer's grasp of Him." — slightly homiletical restatement.

**LOST HEDGES**
- The ־מוֹ count ("six times… all inside the judgment block") is presented as fact — and it checks out (לָמוֹ v.9; הניעמו, והורידמו v.12; פימו, שפתימו v.13; ואינמו v.14). Properly an anchored claim, not a lost hedge.
