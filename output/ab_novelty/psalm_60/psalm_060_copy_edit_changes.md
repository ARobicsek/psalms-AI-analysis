## Changes
*For exact before/after text, see [psalm_060_copy_edit_diff.md](psalm_060_copy_edit_diff.md).*

1. [2,7] **Introduction**: Changed "against the Greek and Syriac reading קֶשֶׁת" to "against the Greek reading קֶשֶׁת." The original contradicted the later verse-by-verse note that Syriac supports the Masoretic "truth" reading, so it created both a factual error and an internal inconsistency.

2. [9b] **Introduction**: Hedged "It belongs to a recognizable Davidic genre" to "It may belong to a recognizable Davidic genre." The connection to 2 Samuel 1:18 is suggestive, but the evidence presented does not prove the genre claim as established fact.

3. [8,9e] **Introduction — The grammar of the not-yet**: Replaced the technical "cohortative" discussion with the less technical "first-person resolve," and changed "irreversible" to "already presented as fact." Not all the cited forms are straightforward cohortatives, and the perfect marks completed action in viewpoint, not logical irreversibility.

4. [7,9b] **Introduction**: Changed "what the same verbs do to the land" to "what the poem's verbs do to the land," and changed Edom from "legally owned/possessed" to "claimed." The verbs in vv. 4 and 8 are not the same, and the shoe image later supports a claim of ownership more securely than fully established legal possession.

5. [6,9e] **Introduction**: Softened the Qabbani comparison from an "identical collision" to "a similar collision," and removed the misleading phrase "same verbs." The parallel is illuminating but not identical in all respects, and the original wording overstated the correspondence.

6. [9b] **Introduction**: Changed "That a psalm could be assigned for memorization on exactly this basis is the lesson..." to "may be the lesson..." The argument is plausible but not demonstrated strongly enough to be stated as certainty.

7. [8] **Verse 1**: Removed the unnecessary stem label from לְלַמֵּד. The grammatical label added no interpretive value beyond the translation and point already being made.

8. [CITATION FIX] [7] **Verse 2**: Added בְּגֵיא הַמֶּלַח to the 1 Chronicles 18:12 quotation. The original quote omitted words from the middle of the cited verse and therefore was not a contiguous biblical substring.

9. [7,9b] **Verse 2**: Changed "separated by a vowel" to "arising from different root analyses." The LXX's "set on fire" reading reflects a different root interpretation, not merely a vocalic difference.

10. [8] **Verse 3**: Changed "one abrupt imperative" to "one abrupt plea" and removed the stem-name label "Polel," replacing it with "a rare intensive form." The original morphologically called the form an imperative, and the stem name was more grammatical jargon than the audience needed.

11. [CITATION FIX] [7] **Verse 3**: Corrected the citation from Job 12:22 to Isaiah 12:1 and restored the contiguous Hebrew quotation אָנַפְתָּ בִּי יָשֹׁב אַפְּךָ וּתְנַחֲמֵנִי. The original cited the wrong biblical verse and quoted a non-contiguous fragment.

12. [7] **Verse 4**: Removed the claim that the Septuagint's σαλεύω here is present. The Greek form in this verse is not present tense, so the original grammatical support for ongoing collapse was inaccurate.

13. [2,7,9b] **Verse 6**: Rewrote the discussion of לְהִתְנוֹסֵס/נוס and changed Selah from marking the threshold into the oracle to marking the pause before verse 7. The original concordance claim misdescribed the evidence, and the oracle does not begin until verse 8.

14. [7,9b] **Verse 7**: Changed "Solomon's throne-name" to "Nathan's name for Solomon" and hedged "likely the trace" to "perhaps the trace." Jedidiah is not a throne-name, and the communal-adaptation explanation is plausible but not certain.

15. [1,8,9e] **Verse 8**: Replaced "cohortatives" with "first-person resolve forms," changed "a merism for the whole land" to "a pair that spans both sides of the Jordan," and softened "no accident" to "may not be accidental." Not all the forms cited are clean cohortatives, and Shechem/Succoth do not by themselves prove a full merism.

16. [9f] **Verse 9**: Removed the Torah Temimah/Talmud sentence about Machir and מחוקקים. The original introduced an opaque scholarly detour whose relevance to the argument was not explained for the reader.

17. [7,9b] **Verse 10**: Hedged the sandal image from certainty to possibility, clarified Deuteronomy 25:9 as public renunciation of claim rather than property transfer, and softened the Philistia conclusion to subordinate status. The legal-ownership reading is plausible but not exclusive, and the levirate example is not a simple transfer parallel.

18. [7,9e] **Verse 11**: Changed Sela/Petra from "impregnable... no army logistics can breach" to "notoriously difficult to assault," and clarified that נָחַנִי is formally past rather than simply past. The city was historically captured, and the mixed verbal forms are more nuanced than the original wording allowed.

19. [2,9e] **Verse 12**: Changed "lived experience flatly contradicts the divine speech" to "has not yet caught up to the divine speech," and scaled down the absolute language about the Elohistic Psalter and redaction. The oracle is delayed rather than falsified, and the original claims about name-suppression were too categorical for the evidence given.

20. [9b] **Verse 13**: Removed "and an idol" from the שָׁוְא comparison. The paragraph only supplied evidence from the Decalogue's false/empty-oath usage, so the idol claim did not follow from the reasoning presented.
