## Changes
*For exact before/after text, see [psalm_058_copy_edit_diff.md](psalm_058_copy_edit_diff.md).*

1. [9(g)] **Introduction / anatomical section**: Changed "The aborted stillbirth" to "The stillbirth."
   The original phrase was redundant and inaccurate; a stillbirth is not helpfully described as an "aborted stillbirth."

2. [9(e)] **The competence that fails at the one moment it matters**: Changed "the most emphatic phrase Hebrew can build for mastery" to "a highly emphatic phrase of mastery."
   The original wording overstated the claim beyond the evidence; the phrase is emphatic, but "most emphatic possible" was too absolute.

3. [7] **The competence that fails at the one moment it matters**: Changed "the psalm's last word, אֱלֹקִים שֹׁפְטִים בָּאָרֶץ" to "the psalm's closing declaration, אֱלֹקִים שֹׁפְטִים בָּאָרֶץ."
   The cited Hebrew is not a single "last word" but a multi-word closing declaration.

4. [9(b)] **Liturgical — Key verses**: Removed the claim that the confession "acknowledg[es] that the community's own sins helped empower those foes."
   The evidence given only supports the point that the curse is embedded within communal confession, not the further causal claim about empowering enemies.

5. [9(c)] **Verse 1**: Added a hedge: "may hear the psalm's restraint built into its title."
   The intertextual resonance with Isa 65:8 is suggestive, but the original wording stated the interpretive payoff too certainly.

6. [7, 9(f)] **Verse 1**: Recast the LXX etymology note as "a writing-related etymology" rather than saying the translator "read מ as related to כתב."
   The original formulation asserted a specific derivation the paragraph had not adequately demonstrated.

7. [7] **Verse 2**: Corrected the Septuagint discussion by removing the claim that it "rewrote the line in the first person."
   The Greek remains a second-person address; the original statement misdescribed the text.

8. [9(g)] **Verse 4**: Changed "the stillbirth that never leaves the womb" to "the stillbirth that never sees the sun."
   A stillbirth does not, strictly speaking, "never leave the womb"; the psalm's own emphasis is on not seeing the sun.

9. [9(b)] **Verse 5**: Replaced "lifts the serpent out of the barnyard and into something closer to apocalyptic horror" with "slightly heightens the simile."
   The Ezekiel comparison justified noticing a heightened form, but not the stronger conclusion about "apocalyptic horror."

10. [7] **Verse 5**: Removed the claim that אטם is used of "plugging water-channels (2 Chr 32:30)."
    That citation does not use this verb; the original statement was textually inaccurate.

11. [9(e)] **Verse 5**: Changed "that is the whole Mediterranean expectation" to "that is a familiar ancient expectation."
    The original phrasing claimed too broad a cultural scope from a single literary parallel.

12. [8, 9(e)] **Verse 6**: Changed "Hebrew's most emphatic possible construction" to "a highly emphatic construction."
    The technical point about the cognate accusative is useful here, but the original superlative overreached.

13. [1, 9(b)] **Verse 7**: Replaced the "tight chiasm" claim with a simpler framing description: "divine name / destruction verb ... destruction verb / divine name."
    The original structural label compressed the verse too aggressively; the framing is clear, but the chiasm claim was not sufficiently demonstrated.

14. [7] **Verse 7**: Removed the citation "it tears down walls (Isa 22:19)" from the description of הֲרֹס.
    That verse does not provide the claimed example; the citation was inaccurate.

15. [9(g)] **Verse 9**: Changed "end as a slug consuming itself in motion" to "end as a lowly mollusk consuming itself in motion."
    A snail is not a slug; the original analogy was factually off.

16. [9(c)] **Verse 9**: Hedged the Esarhaddon treaty claim: "There may be an Assyrian backbone here too... The resemblance suggests..."
    Similar wording can support a parallel, but not the stronger assertion that the poet directly "lifted" the curse from the treaty.

17. [9(f)] **Verse 10**: Removed the sentence about a rabbinic pun on שׂער / שֵׂעִיר and Esau.
    The paragraph did not explain the source or the logical steps connecting the pun to the conclusion, leaving the argument opaque.

18. [9(c)] **Verse 11**: Changed "Its mythological source is the Ugaritic Anat" to "It has ancient-Near-Eastern parallels; in Ugaritic myth Anat..."
    The Ugaritic material provides a parallel, but the original wording overclaimed direct derivation.

19. [9(a)] **Verse 11**: Changed "whether any human revenge could ever be that response" to "whether vengeance could ever be that response."
    Psalm 58 speaks of divine vengeance witnessed by the righteous, not straightforwardly of "human revenge"; the original wording blurred that distinction.

20. [7, 2] **Verse 12**: Changed "The same participle" to "The same root" in the comparison with תִּשְׁפְּטוּ.
    The earlier form is a verb, not a participle; the connection is lexical, not a repetition of the same participial form.

21. [7, 9(b)] **Verse 12**: Changed "from its sealed eyes to its final, opened ones" to "from the stillbirth that never sees to the righteous who behold vengeance."
    The psalm speaks of a sealed ear, not sealed eyes, and its seeing-language culminates in v. 11, not in a final image of "opened eyes."
