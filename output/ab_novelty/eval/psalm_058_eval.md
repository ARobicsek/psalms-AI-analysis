## Failure audit

### Commentary A

**OVERREACH**
- *"The Vassal Treaty of Esarhaddon curses the disloyal vassal in similar terms: 'may they melt away like a snail.'"* The specific snail wording is doubtful (Esarhaddon's curses run to wax, water, salt, etc.); the verse is partially hedged ("There may be… too"), but the quoted "verbatim" curse is presented as fact.
- *"the prophetic 'behold' of Isaiah and Amos, not the everyday רָאָה"* — חזה is treated as exclusively prophetic; it is not (cf. ordinary poetic uses). The contrast is overdrawn, and the whole חזה argument leans on it.
- *"His very first blow lands not on the hands… but on the mouth"* — a real interpretive choice stated as a structural fact.
- The anatomical scheme quietly enlists the **eye** as an "instrument of communication." The eye communicates nothing; this is a stretch to keep the triad tidy.

**FALSE PROFUNDITY**
- *"they are the same sentence said twice"* — pleasant cadence restating A1.
- *"To be cancelled is to never see; to be vindicated is to see."* — epigram restating A6.
- *"It is the instrument repossessed."* — restates A5 in fewer words (here it actually earns the cadence, so mild).

**LOST HEDGES**
- The redundant "in their mouth" as a deliberate target-flag is stated as established; it is a defensible reading, not a fact.
- "the same midrashic handling of a borrowed verse" — asserted about the machzor's adjustments.

### Commentary B

**OVERREACH**
- *"every finite verb in those verses belongs to the wicked"* — true only if the cobra's verbs are counted as the wicked's; defensible but stated absolutely.
- *"מִבֶּטֶן belongs to the language of election"* — slightly overstated as a fixed category, though well-anchored (Isa 49:1; Jer 1:5).
- *"BDB notes its likely original sense was serpent-charming specifically"* (לחש) — attribution stated more confidently than BDB warrants.

**FALSE PROFUNDITY**
- *"Evil here is meticulous. It keeps good books."* — punchy but only restates the תְּפַלֵּסוּן gloss.
- *"It withholds the name for five verses precisely so that… the answer to 'where is God?' is… 'here, on earth, judging.'"* — restates B1 (here the restatement is load-bearing, so forgivable).

**LOST HEDGES**
- "Nils Lund noticed the precise architecture" — attribution is plausible but unverified as worded.
- B2's count claim ("exactly three times") is **correct and checkable** (v.7 ×2, v.12 ×1) — a calibration success, noted to its credit.

**v.2 (אֵלֶם crux):** Both treat it fully. B is stronger: it not only lists the three readings but *commits* to "mute" and shows it pays off (the silent judges mirror the deaf snake), turning the crux into the poem's spine.
