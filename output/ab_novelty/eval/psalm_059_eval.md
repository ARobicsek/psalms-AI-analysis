## Failure audit

### Commentary A
**OVERREACH**
- "The phonetics **confirm** that the poet heard the kinship" — the phonetic similarity is real, but "confirm" presents authorial intent as established fact; this is conjecture.
- "a placement later commentators **found hard to justify**" (Perek Shirah) — asserted twice with no source; unsupported.
- "Eight centuries before modern analysis, a liturgist had already **felt the two refrains as a single convertible pair and chosen, deliberately**, the שׁמר pole" — a strong mind-reading claim; the splice is real, the deliberate motive is inference dressed as fact.

**FALSE PROFUNDITY**
- "The change of one suffix, עֻזּוֹ → עֻזִּי, is the change of the whole world." — cadence inflating a (genuine) morphological point already made.
- "a corpse instructs no one." — epigram restating the preceding Rashi gloss.

**LOST HEDGES**
- "the supposed grammatical wreckage **is** the point" — an interpretive reading stated as settled.

### Commentary B
**OVERREACH**
- "a phrase found **nowhere else in the Psalter in exactly this form**" (אֱלֹקֵי חַסְדִּי) — checkable and probably true, but "in exactly this form" does a lot of quiet hedging work to stay safe.
- "invisible to anyone hearing the verse aloud" (עָוֺן/אָוֶן) — slightly self-contradictory: a near-homophone is precisely what *is* heard; the *distinction* is what's lost. The point survives but is loosely worded.

**FALSE PROFUNDITY**
- "The enemies return forever and arrive nowhere; the man they hunted wills one thing… and reaches it." — handsome, but it restates the aspect thesis already stated.
- "God is the constant; what deepens is the singer's grasp of Him." — true, but a summary cadence.

**LOST HEDGES**
- "the sorting **is** the verdict" — the aspect-as-verdict reading is an interpretive thesis worded as fact (though B does supply the supporting data honestly, including the counter-example אָרְבוּ).

B's overreaches are fewer and milder; B repeatedly anchors its strongest claims with checkable counts and acknowledges counter-evidence (the perfect אָרְבוּ inside an "imperfect" thesis).
