# Master Editor Paragraph Outline Prompt (Deprecated)

*Archived from a test session attempting to force the master editor to plan its essay paragraph-by-paragraph before writing.*
*This was removed from the active pipeline because it was decided not to persist it.*

```markdown
You will write THREE sections. But first, plan your essay.

### PRELIMINARY STEP: ESSAY PARAGRAPH PLAN (do NOT include in final output)

Before writing the introduction essay, think through a paragraph-by-paragraph plan. For each planned paragraph, articulate in ONE sentence:
(a) What this paragraph argues or establishes
(b) How it advances your governing thesis

This plan is your internal scaffolding — it should NOT appear in your final output. Use your thinking/reasoning to work through this plan. The purpose is to ensure every paragraph has a clear job and that the essay builds cumulatively toward a single destination, rather than drifting into a series of loosely related observations.

**Example plan (for illustration only — yours will differ):**
1. HOOK: Pose the puzzle of why a death-and-recovery psalm opens daily worship. [Sets up the central question]
2. STRUCTURAL MAP: Lay out the psalm's six-movement architecture. [Gives reader the floor plan]
3. THE SUPERSCRIPTION PUZZLE: Show how commentators have struggled with "dedication of the House." [Establishes the interpretive problem the essay will resolve]
4. MALBIM'S SOLUTION: The house is the body. [Resolves the puzzle and reframes the entire psalm]
5. LITURGICAL LOGIC: Connect body-as-house to daily prayer. [Shows why the community chose this psalm for this slot]
6. THE ARGUMENT FROM PROFIT: Analyze the transactional rhetoric of vv.9-11. [Reveals the psalm's theology of purpose]
7. CLOSING: The two "forevers" — from delusion to gratitude. [Destination the essay has been building toward]

After completing your plan, ask yourself: "Does every paragraph advance a single governing argument? Is there a paragraph that merely displays erudition without moving the argument forward?" If so, revise the plan before writing.
```

And in the checklist before submitting:
```markdown
- PARAGRAPH AUDIT: For each paragraph after the hook, can you state in one sentence what it argues and how it advances the governing thesis? If any paragraph merely displays erudition without moving the argument forward, restructure or cut it.
```
