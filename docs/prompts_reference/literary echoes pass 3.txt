PSALM  — LITERARY ECHOES (Pass 3: Source Verification)

Attached is a literary echoes document for a Psalm. Your job is to AUDIT **EVERY** quotation for accuracy. This is a verification task, not a creative task. Do not audit a sample of the quotations - find and audit them all.

```
For EACH quotation in the attached document, do the following:

1. VERIFY the quotation exists in the attributed work. Search for the text. Check the author, work title, and section/line number.

2. VERIFY the original-language text. Is the Hebrew/Greek/Latin/Persian/German/Russian/etc. text accurate? Are there obvious errors (wrong script, garbled characters, words from the wrong language)?

3. VERIFY the English translation is a reasonable rendering of the original-language text.

4. VERIFY the line/section attribution. Is the quotation actually from the cited canto, act, scene, line, stanza, or section?

=== OUTPUT FORMAT ===

For each quotation, report one of:

✅ **[Author], *[Work]*, [section]** — Verified. [Optional: minor correction, e.g. "line number is 312, not 310"]

⚠️ **[Author], *[Work]*, [section]** — Partially verified. [Explain: e.g. "The quotation is real but comes from Act II, not Act III" or "The Hebrew is correct but the English translation is loose — a more accurate rendering would be..."]

❌ **[Author], *[Work]*, [section]** — Cannot verify. [Explain: e.g. "I cannot confirm this quotation exists in the cited work" or "The Persian text appears garbled" or "This ghazal number does not match standard editions"]

🔄 **[Author], *[Work]*, [section]** — Corrected. [Provide the corrected quotation, language text, or attribution]

=== SPECIAL ATTENTION ===

Pay extra attention to:
- Medieval Hebrew poetry (piyyut, Andalusian verse) — quotations are frequently fabricated or misattributed by LLMs
- Persian and Arabic poetry — divan/ghazal numbers are often wrong; the poetry itself may be real but misattributed to the wrong poet or wrong section
- Chinese and Japanese poetry — characters may be garbled or from the wrong poem
- Line numbers for long works (Iliad, Aeneid, Divine Comedy, Paradise Lost) — these are frequently off by 10-50 lines
- Song lyrics — often paraphrased rather than quoted accurately (note: if the lyrics are substantially correct in meaning but not word-perfect, mark ⚠️ not ❌)

=== PROFANITY FILTER ===

Additionally, flag as ❌ any quotation that contains severe English profanity — specifically the three most-bleeped four-letter words for the sex act, for excrement, and for female anatomy, and their direct cognates or slurs. This is a very narrow filter: historical mild scatology (Rabelais, Chaucer's bawdy, "damn", "hell", "piss", "ass" as mild insult) does NOT trigger this filter. A quotation that would not appear in a mainstream religious commentary for register reasons should be marked ❌ so it is stripped in Pass 4, even if the attribution is factually correct. If a sanitized or radio-edit version of a song lyric exists, mark it 🔄 and provide the clean version.

=== IMPORTANT NOTES ===

- If you are UNSURE whether a quotation is accurate, say so honestly. "I cannot verify this" is a valid and useful answer. Do not guess.
- Do NOT evaluate whether the comparisons are good or illuminating — that is not your job here. Only verify factual accuracy of sources.
- If a quotation is fabricated but the comparison idea is good, say: "❌ Quotation cannot be verified. However, [Author] does address [theme] in [actual work] — consider replacing with a verified passage."
- For well-known works (Shakespeare, Milton, Sappho fragments), you can verify with high confidence. For obscure works, be transparent about your confidence level.
```
