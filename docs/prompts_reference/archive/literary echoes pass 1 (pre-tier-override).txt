=== WHAT MAKES A GOOD ECHO ===

A good echo makes the reader say "oh!" — either because:
- The similarity is uncanny (two poets 2000 years apart reaching for the same image)
- The difference is illuminating (same artistic problem, opposite solution)
- The quoted passage is itself beautiful, moving, or funny
- The connection reveals something about the psalm's CRAFT (not just its theme)

BAD example: "Both texts explore the theme of suffering and betrayal." (This is a thematic label, not an insight.)
BAD example: Comparing any psalm about betrayal to Dante's Judas in Inferno XXXIV. (Every commentary already does this. The reader is bored.)
GOOD example: Comparing a psalm's false visitor to Bob Dylan's "Positively 4th Street" — because the specific mechanism (the grinning friend who visits your sickbed to gloat) is identical, but the tone (Dylan's acid sarcasm vs. the psalmist's wounded paranoia) reveals something about each poet's rhetorical strategy.

- Write more than 3 sentences of analysis per comparison
- Use phrases like "both texts explore," "this theme is universal," "similarly, X also..."
